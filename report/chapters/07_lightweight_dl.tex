\chapter{Lightweight Deep Learning Refinement}
\label{ch:dl}

Layer~3 of the \projectshort{} architecture (\chapref{ch:system}) adds an optional deep-learning stage \emph{after} scene-aware mathematical denoising (\chapref{ch:routing}) and \emph{before} evaluation or ASR ingestion. This chapter specifies the TinyResidualRefiner student network (runK checkpoint), the DeepFilterNet3-based distillation pipeline, training and model selection, benchmark results, and deployment characteristics. The design goal is to transfer a fraction of teacher quality into a compact checkpoint ($\sim$225\,KB, 56\,129 parameters) that runs in milliseconds on CPU, without replacing the interpretable math backbone at inference.

\section{Design Philosophy: Math-First, DL-Assisted}
\label{sec:dl_philosophy}

\subsection{Motivation}

Large end-to-end enhancers such as DeepFilterNet3~\cite{deepfilternet2023} achieve strong perceptual quality but require millions of parameters, non-trivial memory, and GPU-class throughput for comfortable real-time use (\chapref{ch:background}, Table~\ref{tab:method_tradeoffs}). For local ASR preprocessing---where latency, battery, and explainability matter---deploying a full teacher on every utterance is often impractical.

\projectshort{} therefore adopts a \emph{hybrid} strategy:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Math-first backbone} --- routed OM-LSA/MCRA and specialized front-ends provide a fast, inspectable baseline $D_{\mathrm{math}}$ (\chapref{ch:algorithms}).
  \item \textbf{Residual DL correction} --- a student $\mathcal{F}_\theta$ predicts only a bounded multiplicative mask on the STFT of $D_{\mathrm{math}}$, not clean speech from scratch.
  \item \textbf{Offline teacher} --- DeepFilterNet3 generates training targets; it is \emph{not} invoked at inference when refinement is disabled or when only the student is deployed.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Residual-on-Math Formulation}

Let $Y$ denote the noisy STFT and $D_{\mathrm{math}}=\mathcal{G}_{\mathrm{route}}(Y)$ the complex STFT after mathematical enhancement. The student refines
\begin{equation}
  \hat{Y} = M \odot D_{\mathrm{math}}, \qquad
  M = \mathrm{clip}\bigl(1 + \alpha\,\tanh(\mathcal{F}_\theta(\mathbf{X})),\; m_{\min},\, m_{\max}\bigr),
  \label{eq:residual_mask}
\end{equation}
with $m_{\min}=0.2$, $m_{\max}=1.8$, and residual scale $\alpha=0.8$ (training and default inference). Phase is inherited from $D_{\mathrm{math}}$:
\begin{equation}
  \angle \hat{Y}(k,t) = \angle D_{\mathrm{math}}(k,t).
\end{equation}
Magnitude-domain residual learning reduces the dynamic range the network must represent compared to mapping $Y\mapsto \hat{S}$ directly~\cite{hintondistill2015}, while keeping enhancement decisions partially auditable through the routed baseline.

\section{End-to-End Distillation Pipeline}
\label{sec:distill_pipeline}

\paragraph{Stage 1: Routed math outputs.}
\texttt{run\_pipeline.py} produces \texttt{*\_routed.wav} for each benchmark scene using \texttt{choose\_route()} and override config (\chapref{ch:routing}).

\paragraph{Stage 2: Teacher generation.}
\texttt{build\_distill\_pairs.py} invokes the Python DeepFilterNet CLI (\texttt{deepFilter} in conda env \texttt{dfnet311}) with weights under \texttt{models/DeepFilterNet3/}, writing \texttt{*\_teacher.wav} per scene. The backend resolves the dfnet311 executable by full path to avoid shadowing by a Rust \texttt{deepFilter.exe} in the repository root (\chapref{ch:web}).

\paragraph{Stage 3: Pair manifest.}
Each entry in \texttt{distill\_pairs.json} links $(y_{\mathrm{noisy}}, D_{\mathrm{math}}, T_{\mathrm{teacher}}, s_{\mathrm{clean}})$ with aligned file paths. An 80/20 scene split (\texttt{distill\_split.json}) holds out scenes 13--15 for validation. Optional \texttt{augment\_data.py} can expand pairs with synthetic aug scenes for large-scale retraining (PR merge); the deployed runK checkpoint reported here was trained on the 15 original benchmark scenes.

\paragraph{Stage 4: Student training.}
\texttt{train\_student\_distill.py --tag runK} optimizes $\theta$ with mask-distillation, compressed-magnitude, clean auxiliary, and multi-resolution STFT losses for up to 50\,000 steps or 1200\,s.

\paragraph{Stage 5: Refinement inference.}
\texttt{infer\_student\_refine.py}, \texttt{eval\_runK.py}, or the web \texttt{run\_distill\_refine} flag applies \texttt{student\_runK.pt} to routed audio and writes \texttt{*\_distill\_refined.wav} under \texttt{outputs/distill\_refined\_runK/}.

\section{TinyResidualRefiner Architecture (runK)}
\label{sec:architecture_student}

\subsection{Input Representation}

Given routed waveform $d_{\mathrm{math}}(n)$ and noisy $y(n)$, compute STFTs $D_{\mathrm{math}}(k,t)$ and $Y(k,t)$ with $N_{\mathrm{FFT}}=512$, hop $R=128$, Hann window. Log-compressed magnitudes form a two-channel map:
\begin{equation}
  \mathbf{X}(k,t) =
  \begin{bmatrix}
    \log(1+|D_{\mathrm{math}}(k,t)|) \\
    \log(1+|Y(k,t)|)
  \end{bmatrix}
  \in \mathbb{R}^{2\times F\times T},
  \label{eq:student_input}
\end{equation}
where $F=257$ one-sided bins at 16\,kHz.

\subsection{Dilated Residual Stack}

Early prototypes (\texttt{runA}--\texttt{runD}) used a shallow three-layer Conv2D stack (2641 parameters). The selected \textbf{runK} architecture expands capacity with dilated residual blocks (\texttt{TinyResidualRefiner} in \texttt{train\_student\_distill.py}):
\begin{align}
  \mathbf{H}_0 &= \mathrm{ReLU}\bigl(\mathrm{Conv}_{3\times3}^{2\to 32}(\mathbf{X})\bigr), \\
  \mathbf{H}_i &= \mathbf{H}_{i-1} + \mathrm{ResBlock}_{d_i}(\mathbf{H}_{i-1}),\quad d_i \in \{2,4,8\}, \\
  \mathbf{r}   &= \tanh\bigl(\mathrm{Conv}_{1\times1}^{32\to 1}(\mathbf{H}_3)\bigr) \in [-1,1]^{F\times T}.
\end{align}
Each \texttt{ResBlock} applies two $3\times3$ convolutions with dilation $d_i$, enlarging receptive field along frequency without deep recurrence. The predicted mask follows \eqref{eq:residual_mask}.

\label{sec:distill_pairs}

For each scene $s$ in \texttt{noisy\_testset/}, \texttt{build\_distill\_pairs.py} emits \texttt{noisy\_wav}, \texttt{math\_input\_wav} (routed), \texttt{teacher\_wav}, and \texttt{clean\_wav}. Train/validation split: scene01--12 train, scene13--15 validate (\texttt{distill\_split.json}).

\section{Knowledge Distillation Training}
\label{sec:distill_training}

\subsection{Loss Function}

Beyond mask L1 and compressed-magnitude terms, runK adds a multi-resolution STFT loss $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{MRSTFT}}$ over $(512,1024,2048)$ FFT sizes with magnitude and log-magnitude terms. Let $\psi_c(Z)=(|Z|+\varepsilon)^c$ with $c=0.5$. The refinement $\hat{Y}=M\odot X$ minimizes
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{L} =
    \lambda_1 \,\| M - M_{\mathrm{teacher}} \|_1
  + \lambda_2 \,\| \psi_c(\hat{Y}) - \psi_c(T) \|_2^2
  + \lambda_3 \,\| \psi_c(\hat{Y}) - \psi_c(C) \|_2^2
  + \lambda_4 \,\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{MRSTFT}}(\hat{s}, T),
  \label{eq:distill_loss_runk}
\end{equation}
with defaults $(\lambda_1,\lambda_2,\lambda_3,\lambda_4)=(1.0, 0.3, 0.15, 0.5)$ from \texttt{distill\_config.json}.

\subsection{Optimization Details}

Adam, learning rate $5\times10^{-4}$, gradient clipping at norm 1.0, one random 1.0\,s chunk per step. Loop termination at 50\,000 steps or 1200\,s elapsed. runK local training completed \textbf{21\,488 steps} in \textbf{1200\,s} on CPU with validation mask L1 $\approx 0.386$ (\texttt{train\_log\_runK.json}).

\subsection{From runA--runD to runK}

\texttt{hparam\_search\_report.json} documents early runs (\texttt{runA}--\texttt{runD}) on the shallow 2641-parameter network. \texttt{runD} (lr scale 0.7, 1200 steps, 20.1\,s) improved routed RMS on all 15 scenes (mean $\Delta$RMS $=6.2\times10^{-4}$) but left large residuals on chirp and burst scenes. \textbf{runK} increases network capacity, adds MRSTFT loss, and extends training budget. On the same 15-scene benchmark, runK doubles mean $\Delta$RMS ($1.39\times10^{-3}$) with especially large gains on scene09 (chirp) and scene11 (bursts).

\begin{proposition}[Monotonic refinement on benchmark (empirical)]
\label{prop:distill_improve}
Under \texttt{student\_runK.pt} and inference $\alpha=1.0$, distilled refinement satisfies $e_{\mathrm{refined}} \le e_{\mathrm{routed}}$ for all 15 benchmark scenes. Mean RMS improvement is $1.39\times10^{-3}$, more than double the shallow \texttt{runD} student ($6.2\times10^{-4}$).
\end{proposition}

runK closes approximately 44\% of the mean RMS gap between routed math ($0.00837$) and teacher ($0.00596$). Scene12 and scene14 show slightly lower SNR than the legacy runD refined outputs ($\approx -0.3$\,dB) while still improving RMS vs.\ routed---an expected trade-off when capacity is reallocated toward chirp/burst residuals.

\section{Inference and Deployment}
\label{sec:inference}

\texttt{infer\_student\_refine.py} loads \texttt{student\_runK.pt}, applies peak limiting to 0.99 after iSTFT, and writes refined WAVs. On the web platform, \texttt{distill\_refiner.refine\_with\_student()} imports the same \texttt{TinyResidualRefiner} class from \texttt{train\_student\_distill.py}; \texttt{resolve\_distill\_checkpoint()} reads \texttt{selected\_checkpoint} from \texttt{distill\_config.json}. Users enable runK via \textbf{Method = auto} (recommended) plus checkbox \textbf{Run distill refine} (\chapref{ch:web}).

\section{Limitations and Extensions}
\label{sec:dl_limits}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{15-scene training scale} --- runK reported here uses original benchmark pairs; aug-expanded training (9500+ pairs in merged config) is supported but not required for deployment.
  \item \textbf{Routed-input dependence} --- router or override changes require re-distillation.
  \item \textbf{Magnitude-only mask} --- phase copied from $D_{\mathrm{math}}$; heavy reverberation may need complex-domain extensions.
  \item \textbf{RMS-only selection} --- perceptual or WER metrics may rank checkpoints differently.
\end{itemize}

\section{Chapter Summary}
\label{sec:dl_summary}

This chapter presented the runK lightweight deep-learning layer: a 56\,129-parameter dilated-residual TinyResidualRefiner trained with MRSTFT-augmented distillation losses. On all 15 benchmark scenes, \texttt{student\_runK.pt} improves RMS and SNR versus routed mathematical output while adding $\approx 8$\,ms refine latency. \chapref{ch:web} describes web exposure; \chapref{ch:experiments} places results in the full experimental study.
